Aggregate vs Disaggregate Filing Training: Maximizing RL for Pensions

Q1 2026 Rock

Owner: Craig D'Alessio • Quarter: Q1 2026

Objective

Create a clear, practical training portal that explains aggregate vs disaggregate methodologies for buy/sell matching, with specific guidance on how we process public pensions and single tax‑ID entities. The goal is to help account managers explain these concepts simply, apply them consistently, and reduce confusion during client conversations and claim reviews.

Success Criteria (Score 0–100% at end of Q1)

≥70% of targeted AMs complete the training or review the portal
≥6 client conversations supported using the portal content
≥2 examples added from real cases (FIFO, aggregation, or opt‑in)
≤2 open questions unresolved after live demos
Short survey “Was this clear and usable?” with ≥80% positive responses
0 critical errors or misleading examples

Key Actions

  1. Content polish (weeks 1–3)
    Tighten explanations for FIFO vs LIFO, aggregate vs disaggregate, and recognized loss. Add a simple “how to explain it” script.
  2. Examples + visuals (weeks 2–5)
    Add 2–3 real‑world examples, plus one short visual walkthrough for each key concept.
  3. Training + rollout (weeks 4–8)
    Share in Teams, run two short demos, and collect questions for a small FAQ section.
  4. Measure + close (weeks 8–10)
    Quick survey, update weak sections, and publish final version.

EOS / Traction Concepts Applied

  • Rock: The training portal is the main Q1 goal with a clear finish line.
  • Scorecard: The success criteria above are tracked weekly.
  • V/TO alignment: Improves client clarity and supports consistent filings.
  • People/Process: Standard explanations and examples reduce variability.
  • L10 rhythm: Review progress weekly and clear blockers quickly.
  • Issues List: Capture confusing topics or client objections and address them.

Why This Matters

When account managers explain matching and filing decisions clearly, clients trust the process and approvals move faster. It also reduces back‑and‑forth, prevents inconsistent guidance, and helps newer team members ramp faster.

Is This Rock Good?

Short answer: Yes. It solves a real client‑facing pain point and raises consistency across the team.

What’s working: The portal is focused, practical, and easy to reuse in real conversations.

Watch‑outs: Keep the examples accurate and current, and avoid overloading the portal with jargon.
