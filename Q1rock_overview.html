Q1 2026 Rock

Launch and adoption of the FRT Outlook Search Folder Builder

Owner: Catan • Quarter: Q1 2026 (mid-quarter launch – January start)

Objective

Create the "FRT Outlook Search Folder Builder," use AI where it helps to build the best, most useful version of the app, put together simple training, and get it in regular use across Client Services, Claims Ops, Legal, and Leadership. The goal is for people to build useful Outlook Search Folders in under 30 seconds and spend 30–50% less time hunting for time‑sensitive client and case emails (remittances, claim deadlines, settlement notices, rejections, opt‑ins).

Overview (5 Options)

  1. Polish the Search Folder Builder, give teams a short guide, and get it into daily use. The aim is faster Outlook searches for time‑sensitive client work so people spend less time digging for emails and more time responding.
  2. This Rock focuses on making the tool easy and reliable, then rolling it out across Client Services, Ops, Legal, and Leadership. Success looks like quick setup, consistent folder recipes, and measurable time saved on email triage.
  3. We’re finishing the builder, documenting it, and pushing adoption in Q1. The outcome should be consistent search folders across teams and a noticeable drop in time spent hunting for deadlines, notices, and distributions.
  4. Take the existing tool from “useful prototype” to “daily habit.” That means small UX fixes, simple training, and a clear launch plan so anyone can build search folders in under 30 seconds.
  5. Make Outlook search faster for everyone by tightening the tool, teaching it quickly, and getting teams to use shared favorites. The payoff is fewer missed items and quicker responses on client‑critical emails.

Success Criteria (Score 0–100% at end of Q1)

≥60% of target users have tried the tool and saved at least one favorite
≥8 high-value favorites saved and shared across teams
≥20–25% average reported time saved on routine email triage (via survey)
2+ meaningful usability improvements shipped
1-page guide + 8–10 min demo video created and shared
0 critical bugs reported

Key Actions (Remaining ~9 weeks of Q1)

  1. Quick polish & fixes (next 2–3 weeks)
    Remove UI friction • Improve exclusions and keyword accuracy • Test in real Outlook (desktop + web) • Add a "Copy All Favorites" or export option.
  2. Training & documentation (next 2 weeks)
    Create a one‑page internal guide with screenshots • Record a 10–12 min demo video (3–4 real examples) • Add a first‑use tip in the tool.
  3. Launch & adoption (weeks 6–10)
    Host on intranet/SharePoint • Announce in all‑hands, Teams, and a short leadership email • Run two short live demos (15 min) • Encourage sharing favorites in #outlook-search-tips.
  4. Measure & close (last 2 weeks)
    Send a short 3‑question survey (time saved, useful recipes, suggestions) • Apply top fixes • Present results in the Q1 Rocks review (usage, wins, estimates).

EOS / Traction Concepts Applied

  • Rock: This is the main Q1 goal, owned by one person, with a clear finish line.
  • Scorecard: The success criteria are the weekly scoreboard we check each week.
  • V/TO alignment: The work supports faster client response times and smoother operations.
  • People/Process: Short training and a simple folder standard make the process stick.
  • L10 rhythm: Review progress weekly, remove blockers quickly, and keep momentum.
  • Issues List: Track Outlook quirks and user pain points, then fix them in order.

Why This Matters

Less time hunting for emails means faster responses on deadlines, distributions, and rejections. It also frees up time for higher‑value work, creates consistency across teams, and makes a home‑grown tool something people use every day.

Is This Rock Good?

Short answer: Yes—this is a solid Rock. It’s clear, measurable, and tied to a real business pain point.

What’s working: The objective is specific and time‑bound, the success metrics are concrete, and the plan focuses on quick wins (polish, lightweight training, adoption). The phased actions make sense for a mid‑quarter start, and the tool already exists, so the effort is about finishing and driving use—not starting from scratch.

Why it stands out: If adopted, it creates consistency across teams and reduces time spent searching for time‑sensitive client and case emails. That’s meaningful impact for Client Services, Ops, and Legal.

Watch‑outs: The adoption targets are ambitious for a short window, and Outlook search behavior can vary across versions. Keep the Issues List active and test in real mailboxes early.
