    Examples

    Standard Folder Structure (Veteran Guidance)

    Keep one shared structure so anyone can jump into a mailbox and know where to look. Names stay short, specific, and consistent. 

    Inbox\n  FRT → Clients\n    [Client Short Name]\n      01 — Remittance\n      02 — Documentation\n      03 — Case Updates\n      04 — Claims Deadlines\n      05 — Distributions\n  FRT → Team Updates\n    Legal Updates\n    Monitoring Alerts\n    Operations\n    Marketing & Events\n  FRT → Admin\n    Vendor Questionnaires\n    Audit Requests\n    Policies & SOPs

    Veteran Pointers (Keep It Fast)

    The best structure is the one you can scan in five seconds. Fewer folders, predictable names, and a single place for time-critical items. 

    1) Keep “Clients” and “Team Updates” separate.\n2) Prefix with numbers to lock order (01, 02, 03).\n3) Put deadlines and distributions in fixed slots.\n4) Use client short names only (CSIM, NORGES).\n5) Avoid “misc” — rename the folder instead.

    Search Tips (When You Can’t Find It)

    Use quotes, narrow fields, and search within the folder first.

    folder:\"Inbox\\FRT → Clients\\NORGES\" subject:\"Settlement\" hasattachments:yes\nfrom:\"custodian\" \"NORGES\" \"proof of claim\"
